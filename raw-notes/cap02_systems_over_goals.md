# System Over Goals

What about ambitious goals? Should we push ourselves harder? Be more ambitious with targets? Not so fast!
The winners and losers often share the exact same goals. We all wanna be rich, thin and loved by others.

But with high-level goals, enthusiasm burns out within weeks consistently. Even when achieved, goals are just momentary victories that fade.
"Stop fixating on the finish line and start loving the process" is what James advocates for strongly.

Systems trump goals for lasting change every single time. James Clear argues that focusing on systems over goals leads to more sustainable progress.
Goals define the desired outcome but create a success-failure binary that limits you.
Systems focus on the process that drives continuous improvement.

Systems focus on the process that drives continuous improvement daily. When you fall in love with the process itself, the results take care of themselves naturally.
By prioritizing systems, you shift from chasing fleeting achievements completely.

You build identity-based habits that create long-term transformation instead. Winners and losers have the same goals but different systems.
The goal doesn't change your life. The system does over time. You don't rise to the level of your goals at all.

You fall to the level of your systems you've built consistently. Goals are about the results you want to achieve.
Systems are about the processes that lead to those results. Focus on the system and the goals take care of themselves automatically.

Everyone's obsessed with goals when they should build systems! Your goal is just a target, but your system determines where you'll end up.
