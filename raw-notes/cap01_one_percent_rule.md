# The 1% Rule

This whole **1% better each day** thing? It's not just math, it's legit and proven! The **British cycling team** was garbage for decades, never winning anything significant.

Then coach **Brailsford** came in with a simple idea: *tiny improvements everywhere.*

Better pillows for *sleep recovery*. Cleaner trucks for equipment transport. Hand-washing techniques to prevent illness.

Nothing fancy or revolutionary, but the results were absolutely *insane*. They went from *zeros to heroes* almost overnight.

They **dominated** the Olympics and Tour de France completely. Small habits don't look like much on a daily basis, but give them time to compound and they'll transform your entire life.

Making **tiny, consistent improvements** daily compounds dramatically over time.

Instead of seeking *radical change* that burns you out, focus on being **slightly better today** than yesterday.

Start small and specific with one thing. Track your progress consistently. Be **consistent** rather than chasing *perfection* every single day.

As **James Clear** highlights: "**1% better each day** equals **37 times better** after one year!" This is the essence of the **compound effect** in personal growth.

The math is simple but the results are *extraordinary* when maintained consistently over time.

The power of **1% improvements** reveals itself slowly, then suddenly all at once. Most people quit too early because they don't see *immediate results.*

But **compound effects** work like magic when you give them enough time to accumulate into something *extraordinary.*

Think of habits like **compound interest** for your life. Don’t sweat *perfection*, just be consistent.
